About

I began my academic journey in Engineering Physics, but was soon drawn to the conceptual complexity of theoretical physics, which led me to pursue a second Master’s degree in Physics and Astronomy. This culminated in a PhD in Physics at Ghent University, where I specialised in quantum many-body theory and tensor network methods for strongly correlated systems. I subsequently continued as a postdoctoral researcher, further deepening my expertise in large-scale simulations and rigorous model development.

My transition from academia to quantitative finance was driven by the desire to see the models I develop have a more tangible and direct impact. While the analytical mindset that shaped my academic career remains the core of my professional motivation, financial markets provide an environment where modelling decisions translate into immediate, measurable outcomes, tightly linking theoretical precision to practical consequence. This led me to Eurex, where I am currently employed as a quantitative analyst contributing to the development and calibration of pricing models for exchange-traded derivatives.
